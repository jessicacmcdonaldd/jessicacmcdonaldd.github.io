About Me

My name is Jessica McDonald and I am currently a sophomore at UNCC. I am majoring in Computer Science and Math. I have loved music my whole life and my parents have always made sure that music was incorporated in my life somehow since a very young age. I grew up listening to all of my dad's old CDs in the car on road trips and on an old boombox he had. I remember being little and dancing around our house to Dancing Queen by ABBA and Fat Bottomed Girls by Queen. This was probably my first exposure to the music I love today.

Starting in 4th grade I joined band and played all the way through highschool. I love playing music almost as much as I love listening to music. Starting when I got access to the internet (probably sometime in middle school) I started developing my own personal tastes for music which have slowly developed over the years and have ultimately lead me to where I am today. I started making playlists on Spotify and many other platforms in late middle school and I have been doing it ever since. Making a new playlist, discovering new music, and listening to music are some of my favorite things in the whole world. Music has become my life as listening to music as become more accessable. I can now listen to music wherever and whenever I want as long as a have a set of headphones on me. This has led me to have some pretty crazy listening times on my Spotify wrapped.

Listening, learning, and creating music has changed my life and that's why listening to music is my favorite hobby!

Jazz Music

I've recently gotten into jazz music because I started listening to it a lot when I do homework or when I am cooking. Hiroshi Suzuki (鈴木弘) is one of my favorite Jazz artists and he is how I found out about more jazz artists. The specific group of artists or small section of jazz artists I listen to are all Japanese artists from the 1970s. I find that I like their style of jazz music the best. Suzuki, specifically, is a trombonist from Yokohama, Kanagawa, Japan. I am also interested in artists such as Ryo Fukui, Masabumi Kikuchi, Jiro Inagaki. There are many more artists in the 1970s Japanese Jazz category that I am interested in listening to and learning about!

Some songs to listen to ...

  • The whole album Cat (キャット) by Hiroshi Suzuki (鈴木弘) (1976)
  • ペインテッド・パラダイス (Painted Paradise) by Jiro Inagaki and His Soul Band (1975)
  • Scenery -- Early Summer -- I Want To Talk About You -- all songs by, Ryo Fukui (1976)
  • If I Said The Sky Was Falling (スカイ・ウォズ・フォーリング) -- Matrix (マトリックス) -- from the Matrix (マトリックス) EP by Masabumi Kikuchi (and his sixtet) (1969)
